## Delivery notes (universal)

### Volume, pace, pauses
- **First sentence:** slower than you think. The hook only lands if judges actually hear it.
- **Pause after "contained in 3.15 seconds"** — 2 full seconds. Let it breathe. That's your mic drop.
- **End with a *question*** — "Can I show you?", "Questions?" — never trail off.

### Posture
- Stand. Never sit for a pitch. If the venue forces seated, lean forward.
- Hands visible, open palms. No crossed arms.
- Look at the *nodding* judge first — the rest follow.

### Energy
- High on the hook, **calm** during the demo, **high again** on the moat + ask.
- Varying pace is more important than being "dynamic" — it keeps attention.

### What to wear
- Smart casual beats formal in a tech hackathon. Button-down + dark jeans + clean sneakers.
- No lanyards, no convention badges flipped outward during the pitch — it distracts.

### Hand-off (team pitches)
- Pre-agreed hand-off points only. Never mid-sentence.
- Whoever isn't speaking looks at whoever is — never at the laptop or the floor.

### If you freeze
- Take one full breath. Count "one Mississippi" in your head.
- Then say: "Let me show you this live" and go straight to the dashboard. The demo saves you.

### The 3 sentences you should be able to say even if woken up at 3am
1. The one-line pitch (Section 2 of `MASTER_GUIDE.md`).
2. "Contained in 3.15 seconds."
3. "Teleport controls access. StrongDM controls queries. We control API-level behaviour and DPDP liability."
